7. Resolutions and Orders

  • 4.1 Resolution in Support of Teamsters Local 25 (with 10.7)
    • Resolution by Councilors Clingan, Burnley, Ewen-Campen, Scott, Sait, Strezo, Wilson, and Davis in support of Teamsters Local 25 in their labor dispute with Republic Services.
    • Item 10.7: Communication from the Mayor conveying a communication to Republic Services, urging expeditious resolution to contract negotiations.
    • Councilor McLaughlin signed on as a co-sponsor.
    • Councilor McLaughlin sponsored Bridget Quinn, Legislative Director for Teamsters Joint Council 10, to speak.
      • Summary of Bridget Quinn's Comments: Quinn thanked the council for their support. Local 25 represents 450 workers at Republic Services. Demands include higher wages, affordable health insurance, and safe working conditions. Republic Services has refused to bargain in good faith, causing a strike in 14 communities. The strike is due to Republic Services' refusal to pay competitive wages and respect workers.
    • Councilor Strezo commended Teamsters 25 and criticized Republic Services for contempt towards its workforce and customers.
    • Councilor Scott highlighted the resolution's demands for fair wages, affordable healthcare, safe working conditions, and adequate paid time off.
    • Councilor Ewen-Campen requested the administration consider extending municipal tipping access to residential buildings affected by the strike.
    • Both items approved.
  • 10.1 Order for Speed Bump Assessment on Cherry Street

    • Item approved.
  • 10.2 Resolution Condemning Harvard's Attack on its Graduate Student Union
    • Councilor Burnley moved to replace the original resolution with a revised version. Motion approved.
    • Revised resolution language read into the record.
      • The Somerville City Council strongly opposes actions by Harvard to strip union protections from workers in science research, urges Harvard to undo these actions and engage with the demands of the Harvard Graduate Students Union on issues from fair pay to protections from harassment and discrimination to resources for non-citizens.
    • Councilor Burnley spoke about the importance of recognizing the dignity of workers and the dangers of defining workers in a way that removes their rights.
    • Councilor Burnley sponsored Dorothy Manovich, a Somerville resident and member of the Harvard Graduate Student Union, to speak.
      • Summary of Dorothy Manovich's Comments: Manovich thanked the council for the resolution. She has been able to live a good life in Somerville because of her union. Harvard University is attempting to strip union rights from 973 workers and partially from an additional 1,000 workers. These workers will lose wage protections, harassment and discrimination protections, and access to emergency funds.
    • Councilors Wilson, McLaughlin, Scott, Strezo, and Mbah signed on as co-sponsors.
    • Councilor Wilson thanked Councilor Burnley and Dorothy for their leadership.
    • Councilor McLaughlin spoke about the exploitation of student labor in academia and the importance of student unionization.
    • Councilor Scott urged Harvard to de-escalate the conflict and recognize that graduate students are not the enemy.
    • Councilor Strezo thanked everyone for speaking and reiterated support for the union.
    • Item approved.
  • 10.3 Resolution in Support of Public Workers' Right to Strike
    • Resolution by Councilor Burnley in support of certain public workers' right to strike.
    • Councilor Burnley spoke about the fundamental right of workers to withhold their labor.
    • Councilor Burnley sponsored Jim Kaplan, Marion Walls, and Collette Barard to speak.
      • Summary of Collette Burrard's Comments: Burrard, a public school teacher, described her local's strike in Andover due to the school committee's refusal to negotiate. She urged support for the resolution to give public sector workers the ability to withhold their labor.
      • Summary of Jim Kaplan's Comments: Kaplan, a resident and Mass Teachers Association member, discussed the historical division between private and public sector labor rights. He argued that engaging the working class through the labor movement is essential for the Democratic Party.
      • Summary of Mary and Wallace's Comments: Wallace, a regional vice president for SEIU 509, explained that public sector workers are often held "at gunpoint" because they lack the ability to strike. She emphasized that contracts are not just about wages but also about working conditions and fighting for the community.
    • Councilors Wilson, Scott, Mbah, and McLaughlin signed on as co-sponsors.
    • Councilor McLaughlin spoke about the history of labor and strikes and the risks workers take when striking.
    • Councilor Wilson noted that good contracts avoid public sector strikes.
    • Councilor Scott highlighted that public sector workers often take lower pay because they believe in public service.
    • Councilor Mbah thanked the speakers and emphasized the importance of fighting for people.
    • Councilor Davis noted that the resolution expresses support for something already before state representatives.
    • Item approved.
