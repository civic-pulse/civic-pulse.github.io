Meeting Minutes: Somerville Zoning Board of Appeals

Meeting Date: August 20, 2025 Governing Body: Somerville Zoning Board of Appeals Type of Meeting: Regular Meeting Attendees: * Board Members: Susan Fontano (Chair), Brian Cook (Acting Clerk), Ann Fullerton, Zach Zaremba, Cecilia Daglian * Staff: Kit Luster, Madison Anthony, Christian Had to Know

Executive Summary

The Somerville Zoning Board of Appeals convened on August 20, 2025, to approve previous meeting minutes, receive a presentation on the Draw 7 Park improvements, and address two public hearing items. The Board approved the minutes from July 16, 2025, and August 6, 2025. A presentation on the Draw 7 Park project, focusing on Chapter 91 permitting for beach nourishment, was received, and the Board approved signing the necessary documentation. The public hearing for 872 Broadway was continued to September 17, 2025, at the applicant's request. The public hearing for 8 Melvin Street, continued from July 16, 2025, was also continued to September 17, 2025, to allow the applicant to provide further documentation and explore additional modifications to address zoning compliance issues related to dormer construction.

Draw 7 Park Signature

  • Presentation: Devin Herrick, a wetland scientist with Weston and Sampson, presented on the Draw 7 Park project.
    • Project Location: Adjacent to Assembly Row and the Amelia Earhart Dam.
    • Project Scope: Includes updated site amenities, utilities, pedestrian connectivity, flood barrier development (natural berm), creation of a living shoreline, and increased community access to the Mystic River Reservation Greenway.
    • Environmental Context: Site is at the confluence of tidal and non-tidal resources.
    • Existing Conditions: Wide open space with a fishing pier and small parking area.
    • Proposed Conditions: Open lawn spaces, flexible seating, accessible pathways, shade structure, improved parking, extensive planting plan, and improvements to the shoreline and fishing pier.
    • Chapter 91 Permit: The presentation focused on the Chapter 91 (Public Waterfront Act) permit, which protects filled and flowed tidelands.
    • Beach Nourishment: The project includes beach cleanup and nourishment, adding approximately 397 cubic yards of matching grain size material (maximum 3 inches deep) to the beach area to remove debris and improve accessibility.
    • Permit Requirement: A planning signature and a zoning signature are required as part of the standard Chapter 91 process.
    • Timeline: Construction has begun and is expected to wrap up within the year.
  • Board Questions/Discussion:
    • Cecilia Daglian inquired about the oval areas on the plan, which were described as a large circular lawn space surrounded by naturalized plantings, with pathways connecting to seating areas, shade structures, and the fishing pier.
    • Susan Fontano expressed support for preserving natural sites within the city.
  • Motion: To approve a signature from the Somerville Zoning Board of Appeals for Chapter 91 approval for Draw 7 Park improvements to facilitate the Department of Conservation and Recreation permitting process with the Department of Environmental Protection.
  • Mover: Brian Cook
  • Seconder: Zach Zaremba
  • Vote:
    • Cecilia Daglian: Aye
    • Ann Fullerton: Aye
    • Zach Zaremba: Aye
    • Brian Cook: Aye
    • Susan Fontano: Aye
  • Outcome: The motion carried unanimously (5-0).

8 Melvin Street (Continued from July 16, 2025)

  • Applicant: Rob Passione (Architect) and the homeowner.
  • Background: The application concerns a dormer at the kitchen area on the third floor, which previously had three zoning code violations. The Board had indicated that the original configuration would not be approved.
  • Proposed Modifications:
    • Shift the dormer wall by 35 inches, reducing the dormer width from 16 feet to 13 feet 1 inch. The original approved drawings had a width of 11 feet 7 inches.
    • The applicant believes the new dormer meets the 50% of total dormer ridge length requirement.
    • Two violations remain: dormer separation (increased from 1 foot 11 inches to 4 feet 10 inches) and window ratio (solid wall over 36 inches).
  • Public Comment:
    • Speaker: [Name not provided in transcript], [Address not provided]
    • Summary: Questioned how the project meets the first condition for a hardship variance, specifically regarding a compelling condition related to the building.
    • Direct Quote: "I'm just curious about how this might meet the first condition for a hardship variance after looking at the analysis in the staff report regarding that as to whether there's some compelling condition relating to the building um and how it could be that um it would be necessary to to um construct this in this way so that's about all I have to say I'm just curious thanks"
  • Staff Response (Madison Anthony): Stated that there is nothing about the site that gives it special circumstances to qualify for a hardship variance.
  • Board Discussion:
    • Ann Fullerton: Acknowledged the significant change in removing one hardship request but continued to struggle with the first criterion for a hardship variance. Noted the Board's past willingness to work with residents making changes.
    • Zach Zaremba: Agreed with Ann Fullerton, noting the significant changes and the applicant's good faith efforts.
    • Susan Fontano: Expressed concern about projects proceeding without proper adherence to regulations, emphasizing the need for fairness to those who follow rules.
    • Cecilia Daglian: Expressed concern about the "unbalanced windows" and suggested eliminating the left-hand window. Questioned why the dormer couldn't be slimmed further, noting the impact on the kitchen island. Suggested that the windows could be made conforming.
    • Brian Cook: Stated difficulty in stretching Criterion 1 and Criterion 2 of the hardship variance criteria to fit this situation. Highlighted that the issue stems from design decisions, not inherent problems with the building or zoning.
  • Applicant/Architect Response:
    • Homeowner: Explained that construction occurred during COVID-19, making oversight difficult. Stated that they have done everything possible to comply without completely reconfiguring the kitchen. Noted financial hardship due to inability to sell units.
    • Rob Passione (Architect): Explained that the original design aimed for a "market rate kitchen" in the third-floor unit, which necessitated the dormer size. Acknowledged that site conditions are not the primary factor but argued that the existing structure made it difficult to put a kitchen there, leading to the contractor's decision to move it.
    • Brian Cook: Reiterated that the Board considers unusual structural features for Criterion 1 but was not convinced by the rationale provided.
    • Ann Fullerton: Suggested that the refrigerator could potentially be tucked into the eave, and windows could be relocated to make a 12-foot dormer work, making it difficult to support the variances.
    • Zach Zaremba: Requested clearer justification from the applicant on why the first condition for a hardship variance should be satisfied, specifically regarding what makes the lot, building, or order irregular.
    • Board Consensus: The Board suggested the applicant provide:
      • Pictures of the existing exterior and interior (kitchen) to illustrate how modifications inside could address exterior compliance.
      • Further documentation and brainstorming to demonstrate the "unique or unusual condition" of the structure that necessitated the changes and resulted in zoning violations.
  • Request for Continuance: The applicant requested a continuance to allow time to gather more documentation and explore further modifications.
  • Submission Deadline: For the September 17, 2025, meeting, materials should be submitted by noon on September 10, 2025. Madison Anthony clarified that she cannot perform a full compliance check, which falls under Inspectional Services Department (ISD).
  • Motion: To approve the request for a continuance to the September 17, 2025, Zoning Board of Appeals meeting in the matter of 8-8A Melvin Street.
