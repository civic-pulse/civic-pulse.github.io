Somerville Zoning Board of Appeals Meeting Minutes

Meeting Date: July 16, 2025 Governing Body: Somerville Zoning Board of Appeals Meeting Type: Regular Meeting (Remote Participation) Attendees: * Ursula Susan Fontano (Chair) * Ann Brockleman (Vice Chair) * Ann Fullerton (Acting Clerk) * Zach Zaremba * Brian Cook (Alternate Member) * Cecilia Daglian (Alternate Member) * Kit Luster (Staff) * Steve Carey (Staff) * Madison Anthony (Staff) * Alvaro (Staff)

Executive Summary: The Somerville Zoning Board of Appeals convened remotely to address several zoning matters. Key actions included the continuation of three variance cases for 379 Somerville Avenue and two special permit cases for 14 McGrath Highway. The Board also approved the minutes from two previous meetings. A significant portion of the meeting was dedicated to a 40B change request for the Clarendon Hill project, which was deemed insubstantial and approved. Additionally, an extension for a special permit at 56 Webster Avenue was approved with modified conditions. A hardship variance for 103 Gilman Street was approved to address public safety concerns related to an existing non-conforming structure. Finally, a hardship variance for 181 Pearl Street was approved, allowing for a landscape buffer modification due to an encroaching driveway, with support from the affected neighbor.

8. 103 Gilman Street - Hardship Variance

  • Case Number: ZP25-000043
  • Applicant: Robert Varian (Architect)
  • Request: Seeks relief from SCO 14.1.5.c.11 Point B to expand an existing non-conforming structure further into a rear setback, requiring a hardship variance. The expansion is for 8 inches to make egress stairs code compliant.
  • Background: The property is a three-family unit with existing egress stairs and rear porches in unsafe condition. To replace them and meet current code, the width of the porch needs to extend 8 inches closer to the rear property setback. The existing setback is approximately 3 feet, while 20 feet is required.
  • Board Questions/Discussion:
    • Councilor Ann Fullerton: Noted a discrepancy between the applicant's stated 8-inch extension and the report's 14-inch figure. Kit Luster clarified that drawings show extensions of 5.5 inches (level 1), 4 inches (level 2), and 5.5 inches (level 3). Discussion clarified that the request was for relief for up to 8 inches of further encroachment.
    • Fire Rating: Questioned if fire rating requirements would apply given the proximity to the property line (less than 3 feet). Robert Varian stated he would include fire-rated material if necessary.
    • Councilor Brian Cook: Suggested that the request for 8 inches provides a margin of error, preventing the applicant from needing to return for minor discrepancies.
    • Councilor Ann Fullerton: Expressed concern about the lack of precise documentation for the relief and suggested tying the approval to the specific dimensions on the architect's framing plans (6 feet 3 inches wide).
    • Staff (Kit Luster): Suggested continuing the application to allow for updated plot plans to ensure accuracy, but the Board opted to proceed due to the public safety concern.
  • Public Comment: No members of the public raised their hand to speak.
  • Hardship Criteria Findings (as discussed and adopted by the Board):
    • Criterion 1 (Special circumstances): This is an existing non-conforming structure that already violates the setback. The issue is conforming to current life safety building code for egress.
    • Criterion 2 (Literal enforcement creates hardship): Violating life safety is a hardship.
    • Criterion 3 (Desirable relief without detriment to public good): The change is minor, rebuilding an existing egress condition, and does not affect the public good.
  • Motion: Ann Fullerton moved to approve the hardship variance for 103 Gilman Street, case number ZP25-000043, based on the discussed findings and special circumstances, with the special condition that the permit is granted associated with the architectural drawings dated February 21, 2025, prepared by Robert Varian Architect, specifically plan A-1 where the porch depth is noted, and adopting the criteria discussed in detail.
  • Second: Ann Brockleman.
  • Vote:
    • Zach Zaremba: Aye
    • Ann Brockleman: Aye
    • Cecilia Daglian: Aye
    • Ann Fullerton: Aye
    • Ursula Susan Fontano: Aye
  • Outcome: Motion passed unanimously (5-0).

10. 181 Pearl Street - Hardship Variance

  • Case Number: ZP25-000057
  • Applicant: 181 Pearl Street LLC, represented by Pablo Picker (Owner/Developer) and Paul Miller (Architect).
  • Request: Seeks relief from SCO 10.3.6.A.1 regarding landscape buffer requirements, which prohibits surface parking within the buffer.
  • Background: The project involves building three units on a challenging corner lot. The neighbor at 4 Dana Street has a driveway that encroaches approximately 3 feet 6 inches onto the 181 Pearl Street property. The applicant designed the project to accommodate the neighbor's existing driveway as a gesture of goodwill. This encroachment violates the landscape buffer requirement.
  • Proposed Design: Planters with native species along Pearl Street and Dana Street, permeable pavers, and a bench on the Pearl Street side. The design aims to provide screening and visual amenity, meeting green score requirements.
  • Hardship Criteria Findings (as presented by applicant and adopted by the Board):
    • Criterion 1 (Special circumstances): The neighboring driveway at 4 Dana Street encroaches on the property. 181 Pearl Street is a small corner MR3 parcel (2,024 sq ft), less than one-third the average MR3 size, with proportionally large setbacks (10-foot side setback, 12-foot curb setback) on all four sides, leaving a very small developable area.
    • Criterion 2 (Literal enforcement creates hardship): Prohibiting surface parking in the buffer would require taking back the neighbor's driveway, likely leading to a legal battle and contentious relationship, which constitutes a financial and relational hardship.
    • Criterion 3 (Desirable relief without detriment to public good): Granting relief does not cause detriment to the public good. The proposed landscape design provides screening, visual amenity, meets green score requirements, and buffers the site from neighbors.
  • Public Comment:
    • Dinesh (Owner of 4 Dana Street): Spoke in support of the application, emphasizing that his family has used the driveway for 31 years. Removing access would cause financial burden and he is prepared to contest it legally. He implored the Board to grant the hardship.
  • Board Questions/Discussion:
    • Chair Fontano: Commended the collaboration between the applicant and neighbor. Inquired about the landscape architect and the suitability of plantings for the McGrath Highway traffic conditions. Paul Miller confirmed Ground Inc. as the landscape architect and that species are suitable.
    • Councilor Brian Cook: Noted that the renderings provide a good sense of the appearance and found the proposed solution acceptable, especially with the neighbor's support. Found the criteria satisfactory.
  • Motion: Ann Fullerton moved to approve the hardship variance for 181 Pearl Street, case number ZP25-000057, considering the findings discussed in the meeting, shared in the applicant report, and the staff memo dated July 10, 2025.
  • Second: Ann Brockleman.
  • Vote:
    • Zach Zaremba: Aye
    • Ann Brockleman: Aye
    • Ann Fullerton: Aye
    • Cecilia Daglian: Aye
    • Ursula Susan Fontano: Aye
  • Outcome: Motion passed unanimously (5-0).
