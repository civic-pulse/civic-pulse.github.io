Somerville Planning Board Meeting Minutes - August 7, 2025

Governing Body: Somerville Planning Board Meeting Type: Regularly Scheduled Meeting Meeting Date: August 7, 2025 Attendees: Michael Capuano (Chair), Amelia Aboff (Vice Chair), Jahan Habib (Clerk), Lynn Richards, Michael McNeely

Executive Summary: The Somerville Planning Board convened a regular meeting on August 7, 2025. Key actions included the continuance of two agenda items (59 Bow Street and 379 Somerville Avenue) to the August 21st meeting. The Board approved a signature for a Chapter 91 Beach Nourishment Permit for Draw 7 Park. Additionally, the Board approved site plan approval and a special permit for 181 Pearl Street, with conditions related to material samples and a residential parking sticker prohibition. The Board also discussed 199 Elm Street, requesting further revisions and a continuance to the next meeting. A new agenda item for "Other Business" was formally added to future meetings to allow for general discussion.

2. Draw 7 Chapter 91 Signature

  • Applicant: Amanda Gall, Weston Sampson, on behalf of DCR.
  • Purpose: Request for signature on a Chapter 91 Beach Nourishment Permit application.
  • Project Description: The permit is for beach nourishment at Draw 7 Park, specifically below the high tide line. This includes cleaning debris (glass, bricks, concrete blocks) and replacing it with appropriate grain size material. A robust sampling program will ensure material matches existing beach conditions.
  • Discussion:
    • This is the first time such an item has come before the Board in a long time.
    • The Board's role is to approve the signature in accordance with Chapter 91 of Massachusetts General Law.
    • No public hearing was held as this was a straightforward request.
  • Action: Approved the signature pursuant to Chapter 91.
  • Motion: Moved by Michael Capuano, seconded by Amelia Aboff.

4. 181 Pearl Street (ZP25-000030 and ZP25-000055)

  • Applicant: Pablo Picker (Owner/Developer), Paul Miller (Principal, Neighbor Architects), Curtis Puncher (Landscape Architect, Ground Inc.).
  • Project Description: Development of a three-unit apartment building in the MR3 zoning district. The building is three stories and approximately 3,600 gross square feet.
  • Key Features:
    • Accessibility: Integrated ramp from Pearl Street for zero-step entrance. Ground floor unit is a two-level duplex with a lift for accessibility between levels. Second and third-floor units are walk-up.
    • Materials: Primary cladding is aluminum panel (silversmith color), triangular profile (contemporary interpretation of clapboard siding). Mint green color for balcony spaces. Red brick cladding on planters and site walls. Copper-colored metal panel accent at entry.
    • Landscape: 100% native trees, shrubs, grasses, and perennials. Permeable paving throughout the site. One white oak street tree added on Pearl Street.
    • Green Score: Achieved a score of 0.33 against a target of 0.3.
    • Sustainability: All-electric building.
    • Unit Mix: All three units are two-bedroom units.
    • Parking: No parking provided.
  • Discussion:
    • Accessibility: Councilor Aboff inquired about interior unit accessibility. Paul Miller confirmed the ground-floor unit has a lift, while upper units are walk-up.
    • Parking Stickers: Councilor McNeely raised the issue of residential parking stickers for projects without parking. Madison Anthony (Staff) noted it was not a standard condition in the memo but supported its addition.
      • Proposed Condition: Prohibition of residential parking stickers for tenants.
      • Applicant Response: Pablo Picker stated the design emphasized interior bike parking for residents who would not have cars, aligning with the no-parking assumption.
    • Material Samples: Councilor Aboff suggested a condition for the submission of final material samples, including color samples, to planning staff for review before construction. This was supported by the Board.
  • Public Testimony:
    • Support: Crystal Huff (Ward 5) expressed excitement for the project, appreciating accessibility, compatibility, green score, and the absence of gas pipes. She inquired about the definition of "Group One accessible."
      • Applicant Response: Paul Miller clarified "Group One accessible" refers to an adaptable unit, requiring a fully accessible entrance and wheelchair turning space, with kitchens and bathrooms designed to be modified for full accessibility. Group Two accessibility (fully accessible from day one) is required for buildings with 20 or more units (5% of units).
    • Opposition/Concern: David Hossack (16 Wyndham Street) raised three concerns:
      • Trash Storage: Primary concern was rat mitigation due to existing restaurants and trash storage. He requested a binding condition for internal trash storage in the basement.
      • Parking Permits: Reiterated the need for a condition prohibiting parking permits for tenants.
      • Height Exceedance: Expressed frustration with routine height restriction exceptions, particularly for mechanical penthouses, citing precedent-setting implications for neighbors.
      • Applicant Response: Paul Miller confirmed commitment to basement trash storage for both commercial and residential, noting the passive house design would prevent rats.
  • Action 1 (Site Plan Approval): Approved with conditions as amended in the staff memorandum and discussed tonight.
    • Resolution: ZP25-000030, Site Plan Approval to develop an apartment building in the MR3 zoning district.
    • Motion: Moved by Michael Capuano, seconded by Amelia Aboff.
    • Vote:
      • Jahan Habib: Aye
      • Michael McNeely: Aye
      • Lynn Richards: Aye
      • Amelia Aboff: Aye
      • Michael Capuano: Aye
    • Outcome: Motion passed (5-0).
  • Action 2 (Special Permit): Approved with conditions laid out in the staff memorandum and as discussed tonight.
    • Resolution: ZP25-000055, Special Permit to establish a residential housing use in the MR3 zoning district.
    • Motion: Moved by Michael Capuano, seconded by Amelia Aboff.

5. 199 Elm Street (ZP25-000031, ZP25-000054, ZP25-000066)

  • Applicant: Pablo Picker (Developer), Paul Miller (Principal, Neighbor Architects), Curtis Puncher (Landscape Architect, Ground Inc.).
  • Project Description: Proposed four-story building with ground-floor commercial space (approx. 1,700 sq ft) and three levels of residential apartments (10 units total) in the MR4 zoning district.
  • Key Features:
    • Location: East side of Elm Street, southern end of Davis Square, identified for enhancement in SomerVision.
    • Commercial Space: Designed with folding windows to open in fair weather.
    • Residential Units: 10 units, all Group One accessible. Unit mix: two 2-bedroom, three 1-bedroom, three studios. Two units (one studio, one 1-bedroom) will be affordable.
    • Parking: No car parking proposed. Large bike room for long-term bike parking.
    • Materials: Precast concrete at base (charcoal color), buff-colored brick above. Bright blue glazed brick for stair towers.
    • Transformer: Underground transformer proposed, requiring a step-back and courtyard.
    • Mechanical Penthouse: Special permit requested for mechanical penthouse (elevator bulkhead) exceeding maximum height, necessary for accessible roof deck.
    • Roof Deck: Green roof with amenity space, including private and communal areas, two grills, sinks, harvest table, and vegetable planters (42 inches high, acting as guardrail). 10-inch soil depth for perimeter planting (100% native species). No rooftop HVAC equipment due to integrated heat pump.
    • Landscape: Two hedge maple street trees on Elm Street. Permeable pavers in setback and back patio.
    • Green Score: Achieved a score of 0.357 against a target of 0.3.
    • Sustainability: All-electric building, no gas brought into the building.
  • Discussion:
    • Design Appreciation: Councilor Richards praised the design of the entrance and its integration with Revival Cafe's mural, calling it "exquisitely done."
    • Blank Walls/Public Art: Councilor Richards inquired about plans for murals on the blank side walls, given the emphasis on Davis Square's mural art.
      • Applicant Response: Pablo Picker and Paul Miller acknowledged the discussion, noting the initial design considered future development of adjacent MR4 zoned properties. They expressed openness to commissioning a mural as a temporary use, especially for the Revival Cafe side.
      • Board Consensus: The Board supported a condition for public art (mural) on one or both blank walls.
    • Unit Mix/Accessibility: Councilor Aboff acknowledged the thoughtful design but raised concerns about the unit mix, suggesting that if larger units (e.g., 3-bedroom) are not feasible, at least one of the affordable units could be a Group Two accessible unit.
    • Cornice Detail: Councilor Aboff suggested considering a detail to define the cornice line for aesthetic and contextual reasons.
      • Applicant Response: Paul Miller was open to considering a minimal detail, such as soldier course brick, to complement the building's spirit and Davis Square context.
    • Material Approval: Councilor Aboff requested a condition for material approval, specifically for the accent color of the stair tower.
    • Eversource Transformer: Councilor Aboff inquired about confirmation from Eversource regarding the feasibility of the underground transformer, given its critical role in the design.
      • Applicant Response: Pablo Picker stated discussions with their MEPFP engineer (Zade Engineering) indicated feasibility, but a work order had not yet been submitted to Eversource.
      • Staff Response: Madison Anthony confirmed that if transformer plans changed, it would likely require a plan revision to the Board.
    • Gas-Free Building: Councilor McNeely commended the applicant for proactively designing a gas-free building, especially considering potential restaurant use, aligning with Somerville's carbon-free goals.
    • Trash Mitigation: Councilor McNeely appreciated the "airtight answer" to rat mitigation through internal basement trash storage.
    • Parking Sticker Condition: Chair Capuano requested the standard condition prohibiting residential parking stickers be added.
  • Public Testimony:
    • Support: Crystal Huff (Ward 5) reiterated support for accessibility, green score, and the gas-free design.
    • Opposition/Concern: David Hossack (16 Wyndham Street) reiterated concerns about trash storage (rats), parking permits, and height exceptions.
  • Action: Continued to the August 21, 2025, meeting for further revisions and discussion.
  • Motion: Moved by Michael Capuano, seconded by Amelia Aboff.
  • Written Comment Period: Extended until 9:00 AM on Friday, August 15, 2025. Comments to be sent to planningboard@somervillema.gov.
  • Vote:
    • Jahan Habib: Aye
    • Michael McNeely: Aye
    • Lynn Richards: Aye
    • Amelia Aboff: Aye
    • Michael Capuano: Aye
  • Outcome: Motion passed (5-0).
