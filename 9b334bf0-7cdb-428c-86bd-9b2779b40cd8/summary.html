Agenda Item 7.1: Request of the Mayor requesting the appropriation of $654,019 from the salary contingency account to various police department personal services accounts to fund a collective bargaining agreement with the Somerville Police Superior Officers Association.

  • Matt Siracusa, Labor Counsel, presented the item.
  • The item is to fund the cost items in the Somerville Police Superior Officers Association, a Memorandum of Agreement for a Successor Contract for Fiscal Years 2022 to 2024.
  • The contract mirrors the patrol contract approved in May.
  • Highlights include:
    • 2% Cost of Living Adjustment (COLA) in the first year.
    • 3% COLAs in the second two years.
    • 2% market adjustments in each year of the contract.
    • Civilian detail language from the police patrol contract.
    • Slight bump to hazardous duty pay.
    • Bump to the detailed rate for sworn officers.
  • Councilor Mbah moved for approval.

Agenda Item 7.2: Request of the Mayor requesting the appropriation of $77,400 from the salary contingency account to the Department of Public Works school custodians salaries account to fund a memorandum of agreement with service employees international union local three school custodians.

  • Matt Siracusa, Labor Counsel, presented the item.
  • This is a unique contract due to a wage re-opener provision in the previous contract (fiscal years 23 to fiscal year 25).
  • The wage study revealed that the starting salary was competitive, but there was compression in the salary scale.
  • The Memorandum of Agreement includes:
    • 2% market adjustment added to all steps of the salary scale in fiscal year 25.
    • An additional 2% added to the top step (step 10), making for a 4% total market adjustment in fiscal year 25 for these folks.
    • Effective June 30th, 2025, the wage scale will be recalibrated to have 2% steps instead of 1% steps.
  • Councilor Mbah moved for approval.

Agenda Item 7.3: Request of the Mayor requesting approval to pay prior year invoices totaling $375 using available funds in the human resources, professional and technical services account for arbitration services.

  • Gail, Director of Human Resources, presented the item.
  • The invoices were for services rendered in June 2024 but were not sent until 2025.
  • The invoices were lost in the transition between HR and the Law Department.
  • HR transferred funds to Law, who will now be paying these invoices going forward.
